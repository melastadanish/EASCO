## Section 4 — What Is a Stainless Steel Turnstile?

**H2: What Is a Stainless Steel Turnstile?**

A stainless steel turnstile is a pedestrian access gate built entirely from 316-grade stainless steel — the same material used in ocean-facing infrastructure. Unlike aluminum, which oxidizes and corrodes in coastal salt spray or wet climates, stainless steel frames stay structurally sound for a decade or more without maintenance. EASCO's stainless steel range covers cylindrical speed gates, swing barriers, and integrated biometric terminals. All use the same DC brushless servo motor and crank-link transmission as the aluminum line — meaning identical 0.3-second opening speed and 45 persons-per-minute throughput. The difference is material: buy stainless steel if your site is outdoors, near water, or in a high-vandalism zone. Buy aluminum for indoor facilities.

## Section 5 — Key Features of Stainless Steel Turnstiles

**H2: Key Features of Stainless Steel Turnstiles**

**316-grade stainless steel resists saltwater and constant moisture.** This is the grade used in boat fittings and offshore drilling platforms. A stainless steel gate installed in a coastal plaza stays rust-free for 10+ years without painting, sealing, or special maintenance. Aluminum in the same environment oxidizes and pits within 5–7 years.

**Full-speed throughput even in wet conditions.** Stainless steel frames weigh more than aluminum, but EASCO's stainless gates still open in 0.3 seconds and process 45 people per minute. The DC brushless servo motor and crank-link transmission don't degrade from salt spray or humidity — they're sealed against the environment.

**Anti-tailgating and anti-crush protection work 24/7.** Infrared sensor arrays detect trailing entry in any light condition. Crank-link transmission stops the gate the instant it meets resistance — mechanical safety that doesn't depend on software or power.

## Section 8 — Frequently Asked Questions

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: Why choose stainless steel over aluminum for an outdoor gate?**
A: Stainless steel resists salt spray, constant moisture, and oxidation. A stainless steel gate lasts 10+ years in coastal environments without maintenance. Aluminum gates in the same conditions fail within 5–7 years.

**Q: How fast do stainless steel turnstiles open?**
A: EASCO's stainless steel gates open in 0.3 seconds, processing up to 45 people per minute per lane — identical speed to the aluminum range. Speed doesn't depend on material; it depends on the DC brushless servo motor and crank-link transmission.

**Q: Is stainless steel weatherproof?**
A: Yes — 316-grade stainless steel is used in ocean-facing infrastructure because it resists saltwater corrosion. EASCO stainless turnstiles are suitable for outdoor and indoor installation.

**Q: Can face recognition be integrated into a stainless steel turnstile?**
A: Yes — several models in the stainless steel range include built-in biometric readers. Face, card, and QR code access mount directly into the gate without a separate controller. Integrated models support Wiegand and RS485 interfaces.

**Q: Do stainless steel gates need painting or sealing?**
A: No — 316-grade stainless steel doesn't rust or corrode. No painting, sealing, or chemical treatment required. Occasional rinsing with fresh water in coastal zones is recommended.
