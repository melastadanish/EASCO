### SECTION 2: What Makes Construction Site Access Control Different

**H2: No Fixed Lobby, No Controlled Climate**

An office building has one or two controlled entrances. A construction site has multiple access points that shift as the project progresses, exposed to dust, rain, temperature swings, and constant equipment movement. Standard indoor-rated hardware fails faster in these conditions. A single fixed terminal at a front gate also misses workers entering through a side access point or a different zone entirely.

Attendance accuracy carries more weight on a construction site than in most office settings. Payroll, safety headcounts during an evacuation, and legal liability for who was on site all depend on an accurate record, not a self-reported sign-in sheet.

> Construction site access control needs IP65/IP67-rated hardware to survive dust and weather, biometric verification to prevent buddy punching on payroll, and, where a sober-only policy applies, combined alcohol and identity checks at the point of entry rather than relying on the honor system.

### SECTION 3: Four Considerations for Site Attendance Systems

**H2: What to Check Before Choosing Hardware for a Job Site**

1. **Environmental rating:** Confirm the IP rating on any terminal considered for outdoor use. IP65 covers dust and water jets. IP67 adds temporary submersion resistance, useful for equipment that gets dropped or rained on.
2. **Portability for spread-out sites:** A fixed terminal only covers one entrance. A handheld, battery-powered terminal with 4G connectivity covers a site without a fixed network.
3. **Buddy punching prevention:** Biometric verification (face recognition) ties attendance to a specific person, unlike a card that can be handed to a colleague.
4. **Sober-only policy enforcement, where applicable:** Legal and safety requirements around alcohol testing vary by jurisdiction and site type. Confirm what's actually required before specifying equipment for it.

### SECTION 6: FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: What IP rating do I need for outdoor construction site equipment?**
A: IP65 covers dust and water jet resistance for most covered or semi-covered installations. IP67 adds temporary submersion resistance, useful for handheld devices exposed to rain or accidental drops.

**Q: How do I stop workers from clocking in for each other?**
A: Biometric verification, such as face recognition, ties each clock-in to a specific enrolled person rather than a card or PIN that can be shared.

**Q: Is alcohol testing before clock-in legally required on construction sites?**
A: Requirements vary significantly by jurisdiction and project type. Confirm the specific legal and safety requirements for your site before specifying alcohol testing equipment.

**Q: Can attendance be tracked away from the main site entrance?**
A: Yes. A handheld, battery-powered terminal with 4G connectivity covers a loading dock, yard, or remote work zone without needing a fixed network connection.

**Q: How does a combined alcohol and face recognition terminal work?**
A: A worker scans their face and breathes into the sensor in one motion. Clock-in only completes once both checks pass.
