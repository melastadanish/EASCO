**Stainless Steel Right-Angle Swing Gate Product Specifications**
**I. Product Introduction**
The stainless steel right-angle swing gate is a high-end intelligent access control device for pedestrian passages. The entire unit is precision-crafted from SUS304 stainless steel, with corners processed using a right-angle cutting technique. Its bold, angular lines and clean, streamlined design exude industrial strength and modern aesthetics. Equipped with an integrated DC servo and planetary gear reducer drive system, it delivers powerful performance, rapid response, and smooth, low-noise operation, making it ideal for high-intensity, high-frequency traffic scenarios. The device supports bidirectional access control. The swing gates are available in either acrylic or tempered glass, and the passage width can be flexibly customized. It features built-in infrared anti-pinch protection, fire alarm integration, power failure protection, and remote backend management. Combining rugged durability, powerful performance, and intelligent control, it is the ideal choice for demanding environments and high-traffic scenarios.

**II. Application Scenarios**
1. Access Control: Orderly one-way or two-way passage for pedestrians, large suitcases, small carts, and lightweight non-motorized vehicles;
2. Model Types
 Single-mechanism: A single, independent passage lane, suitable for entrances and exits with small to medium traffic volumes;
 Dual-mechanism: Parallel dual lanes for traffic diversion, designed to handle high-traffic scenarios during morning and evening rush hours;
3. Integrated Peripherals: Facial recognition terminals, IC/ID card readers, QR code scanners, ID card readers, palm print recognition, wireless remote controls, audible and visual alarms, and fire alarm integration modules; comes with a free PC management platform and WeChat mini-program;
4. Suitable Locations: Mid- to high-end access control venues such as commercial office buildings, industrial parks, residential communities, tourist attractions, school campuses, subway stations, airport waiting areas, enterprises and public institutions, and conference centers.
