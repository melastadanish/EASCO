## Competitor Profiles

### ZKTeco

**URL:** zkteco.com
**Position:** Global access control and time attendance market leader. Largest volume player, ~3,500 employees, dual HQ (Dongguan + Xiamen).
**Products:** Fingerprint + face + palm vein readers (PV10M/PV10R modules, PA10 hybrid terminal), time attendance terminals, alcohol detection (G4L + USB Alcohol Tester), full turnstile range (swing/flap/tripod/full-height, Mars Pro speed gates), LPR/ANPR (LPRS1000, E-LPRC500), parking systems (SPB Pro).
**⚠️ CORRECTED TWICE (2026-07-08) — DO NOT use bullet lists from either prior version in published copy.** First correction: ZKTeco does sell palm vein hardware and has an established OEM/ODM division. Second correction: ZKTeco also has a combined turnstile+biometric+LPR range AND a combined alcohol+attendance product — neither is unique to EASCO. The "airport kiosk / FIDS" line from the first correction was never actually verified either way — treat as unconfirmed, not disproven.
**What's still a genuine, verified EASCO angle after two research passes:**
- EASCO's iris FAR (1-in-10,000,000 / 0.00001%) is a specific, publicly-stated figure — no equivalent ZKTeco iris product was found across three separate searches. This is EASCO's strongest remaining specific claim.
- EASCO's ESFAT is a single integrated alcohol+face device; ZKTeco's equivalent is a terminal plus a separate USB attachment. A real design difference, not a capability gap — don't oversell it as an advantage.
- EASCO operates a single, smaller Shenzhen facility (~60+ employees) vs. ZKTeco's much larger, dual-HQ operation (~3,500 employees) — framing depends on audience: some buyers value scale, others value a smaller factory's direct engineering access.

**What ZKTeco has that's genuinely stronger (be honest about this):**
- Larger scale, longer OEM/ODM track record (25+ years cited), broader existing dealer network.
- More granular published specs in places EASCO doesn't separately publish: face FRR (≤0.001%), LPR accuracy (LPRS1000: >99.7%, <200ms).

**How to position against ZKTeco (revised, conservative — second pass):**
> "ZKTeco is a large, established manufacturer with real OEM/ODM, palm vein, and a combined turnstile+biometric+LPR range — don't claim otherwise. EASCO's specific, defensible edge is a publicly documented 1-in-10,000,000 iris FAR, which no ZKTeco iris product was found to match or offer."

### Hikvision Access Control

**URL:** hikvision.com/en/products/Access-Control-Products/
**Position:** Camera-first brand that extended into access control. Massive global distribution, and one of the industry's largest OEM/white-label suppliers.
**Products:** Face recognition terminals (MinMoe series — Value/Pro/Ultra), fingerprint terminals, card readers, flap barriers. Some terminals support iris as an auth method (e.g. DS-K1T671M).
**⚠️ CORRECTED 2026-07-08 — DO NOT use the old bullet list below this line in any published copy. It was factually wrong: Hikvision does offer OEM/white-label at scale and does list iris as an authentication method on select terminals.**
**What's still a genuine, verified EASCO angle:**
- EASCO's iris FAR (1-in-10,000,000) is more specific than anything found for Hikvision's iris-capable terminals in this research pass.
- EASCO is a specialist access control manufacturer, not a camera company that added access control — a genuine positioning difference, though not a capability gap.
- Geopolitical procurement restrictions on Hikvision products exist in some markets (US, Australia, UK government) — this is publicly documented and safe to reference generally, though specific current restrictions should be verified per market before citing in copy.

**How to position against Hikvision (revised, conservative):**
> "Hikvision brings camera-industry scale to access control, including real OEM capability and iris-capable terminals — don't claim they lack these. EASCO's specific edge is a specialist focus on access control hardware and a publicly documented 1-in-10,000,000 iris FAR."

### Suprema

**URL:** supremainc.com
**Position:** Premium South Korean biometrics brand. Known for fingerprint + face, high accuracy, enterprise positioning.
**Products:** Face recognition, fingerprint readers, access control software.
**Weakness vs EASCO:**
- Korean manufacturing = higher price point.
- No palm vein, no iris commercial products.
- No OEM/ODM — brand-only positioning.
- No turnstile gates or vehicle access in core range.
- No airport / transit solutions.
- Less competitive on price for volume orders.

**How to position against Suprema:**
> "Suprema is positioned for high-end enterprise. EASCO is factory direct from Shenzhen — same biometric accuracy, broader product range including palm vein and iris, with OEM capability and pricing that makes large deployments viable."

### Generic Alibaba Suppliers

**Position:** Trading companies (not manufacturers) reselling factory products with no accountability.
**Weakness vs EASCO:**
- Cannot audit the factory because there is no factory.
- Quality is inconsistent order-to-order.
- No after-sales support.
- Certifications are often self-certified or purchased.
- No OEM/ODM — they cannot customise what they don't manufacture.

**How to position against Alibaba traders:**
> "Most access control listings on Alibaba are trading companies — they buy from the same factories you could source directly, with a margin added and accountability removed. EASCO is the manufacturer. Same address, same team, same production line for 20 years. Factory audit available."
