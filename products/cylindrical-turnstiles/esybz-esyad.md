### SECTION 3 — What Is the ESYBZ / ESYAD?

**H2: A Compact Speed Gate for Spaces a Full-Size Barrier Won't Fit**

The ESYBZ, marketed in China as the 水滴圆柱速通门 (water-drop cylindrical speed gate), reduces the footprint of a standard speed gate through an integrated mold body made from aircraft-grade aluminum alloy. A registered design patent covers its double-movement mechanism. The ESYAD is an alternative base body offering the same throughput and mechanism, configured with a card reader instead of the LCD+Face terminal. Both bodies can be configured with a remote controller for basic access, a card reader for card-only access, or the ESYBZ LCD+Face variant's onboard Android terminal for full biometric access.

### SECTION 6 — How It Works

**H2: From Card Tap or Face Scan to Passage in Under a Second**

1. A person presents credentials — a card tap, a face scan on the LCD+Face variant, or a remote-controlled release.
2. The control system verifies the credential against the access database.
3. On a valid match, the door opens in 0.3 seconds, guided by the crank-link transmission mechanism.
4. Anti-pinch sensors monitor the door throughout the cycle, reversing on contact resistance rather than continuing to close.

**Q: What's the difference between ESYBZ and ESYAD?**
A: Both share the same gate mechanism and throughput. ESYBZ and ESYAD are alternative base bodies — configuration (card reader vs. LCD+Face Android terminal) determines the final model name.

**Q: Is the gate body rated for outdoor installation?**
A: The gate body's IP rating is not confirmed on the manufacturer spec sheet — do not assume IP65 for outdoor use. The LCD+Face terminal itself is IP65-rated. Contact EASCO to confirm gate body suitability for a specific outdoor site.

**Q: Can the passage width be customized for wheelchair access?**
A: Yes. Passage width is customizable from 600mm up to 1,500mm.

**Q: What stops the door from injuring someone if it closes on them?**
A: Anti-pinch sensors detect contact resistance and reverse the door immediately, rather than continuing to close.

**Q: Does the LCD+Face variant need a separate access control box?**
A: No. The LCD+Face variant runs an onboard Android terminal handling face, card, QR code, and password access without an external controller.
