## 8. How It Works

**Eyebrow:** HOW IT WORKS

**H2:** 4 Steps. From Iris Scan to Verified Access.

**Subtitle:** Your MTH8 verifies identity using the world's most secure biometric.

**Step 1 — Iris Recognition**
User approaches the MTH8 at 30–50cm distance. The display prompts "Look into scanner." The near-infrared camera captures the iris pattern in detailed high-resolution. The recognition engine compares against the 50,000-pattern database in < 2 seconds. A match is confirmed; the user's name appears on screen.

**Step 2 — Face Recognition (Automatic)**
Simultaneously or immediately after, the face recognition camera captures the user's facial features at 0.5–3 metre range. The system compares against the 50,000-face database. Face match is confirmed. Both biometrics verified.

**Step 3 — Dual-Match Confirmation**
The terminal displays "Iris: MATCHED | Face: MATCHED" with the user's name and access level. Both identity verifications are now complete. The access decision is made. The system logs the timestamp, biometric scores, and access grant/deny status.

**Step 4 — Relay Activation & Logging**
If both iris and face match, the MTH8 sends a relay signal to your door strike or barrier gate, unlocking entry for 1–2 seconds (configurable). The access event is logged to your building security system via Ethernet/WiFi/RS-485. > 1,000,000 local records ensure no access event is lost, even during network outage.

## 9. FAQ

**Eyebrow:** COMMON QUESTIONS

**H2:** What People Ask Before Installing MTH8

**Q1: What's the difference between iris and retina recognition?**
**A1:** Iris is the colored ring around the pupil; retina is the blood-vessel pattern at the back of the eye. Iris recognition is non-invasive (you look at the camera), while retina scanning required close contact and was older technology. MTH8 uses iris recognition—safer, faster, and more accurate.

**Q2: Does iris recognition work on people with glasses or contact lenses?**
**A2:** Yes. The iris itself remains unchanged; glasses and contacts don't interfere with the IR imaging. The MTH8 can read iris through clear and colored contacts. Sunglasses must be removed (same as any light-based biometric).

**Q3: How do we enroll 50,000 people into the MTH8 database?**
**A3:** Enrollment is fast: typically 10–20 seconds per person (iris + face capture). For 50,000 people, allocate 100–200 staff-hours for enrollment over a few weeks. The MTH8 can enroll to local storage, or export to your central identity management system for backup and redundancy.

**Q4: Can the MTH8 work in extreme weather—sand storms, heavy rain, snow?**
**A4:** The IP65 rating protects against dust and water ingress. Extreme sand storms (visibility < 10 metres) may require the user to remove protective eyewear to expose the iris. Heavy snow won't affect performance. The system is designed for harsh outdoor conditions.

**Q5: What's the recognition speed difference between iris and face?**
**A5:** Iris recognition takes < 2 seconds; face recognition takes 200–300ms. The MTH8 typically shows iris results first, then face. If lighting favors face (bright outdoor), the terminal may show face match in < 1 second total, then confirm iris as secondary.

**Q6: What lead time for MTH8 units?**
