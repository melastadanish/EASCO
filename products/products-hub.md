### SECTION 2 — Product Category Grid

**H2: Match the Category to the Entrance, Not the Other Way Around**

#### [Flap Barrier / Speed Gate Turnstiles](/products/speed-gates/)

Nobody gets crushed at the door. Crank-link transmission with magnetic-sensitive limit control stops the flap barrier the instant it meets resistance — mechanical protection, not a software timeout. Lobby and staff entrances where a swinging arm won't fit are the target install.

#### [Tripod Turnstiles](/products/tripod-turnstiles/)

One rotating arm, one person, no ambiguity about who passed through. Tripod turnstiles suit staff entrances and low-to-medium traffic lobbies where a compact footprint matters more than throughput. Mechanical fail-safe rotation means the arm still turns during a power outage.

#### [Cylindrical Turnstiles](/products/cylindrical-turnstiles/)

A packed lobby clears in seconds, not minutes. The ESYBZ and ESYAD cylindrical gates process 45 people per minute and open in 0.3 seconds. That's fast enough during a fire drill, with no bottleneck at the door. Passage width adjusts from 600mm to 1500mm. Anti-tailgating sensors re-close the gate before a second person can slip through behind an authorized entry. One-way or two-way configuration confirmed before shipping.

#### [Android Turnstiles (Face + QR + Card)](/products/android-turnstiles/)

One gate reads four credential types with no separate controller box. The LCD+Face variant of the cylindrical gate runs Android onboard a 1920×1080 IPS display. Face, palm, card, and QR credentials all read at the same lane — what most competitors ship as gate-plus-reader-plus-controller.

#### [Face Recognition Terminals](/products/face-recognition/)

99.98% recognition accuracy at 200–300ms per scan means a queue at the door clears without anyone standing there twice. Recognition distance runs 0.5–3 metres, and the standard model stores 50,000 faces onboard. Financial-grade liveness detection rejects photos, screen replays, and silicone masks. IR illumination reads a face in complete darkness. IP65-rated models handle an uncovered entrance in Shenzhen humidity, and the housing runs -25°C to +75°C without extra cooling.

#### [Handheld Face Recognition Terminals](/products/handheld-face-recognition/)

Recognition doesn't stop at the wall-mounted terminal. The ESR366, ESR51, ESR860, and ESR366C move it onto the warehouse floor, the retail aisle, and the job site — reaching checks a fixed terminal can't. The ESR366C reads HF flexible label cards through a built-in reader. The ESR51 is three-proof rugged for outdoor field use. The ESR860 prints a receipt or ticket on the spot.

#### [Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) Systems](/products/anpr-systems/)

Partial occlusion and low light don't stop the ES-T02/T03/T04 plate recognition camera from reading a number plate. Night vision handles the same lane after dark with no separate hardware. A 30,000-hour MTBF (mean time between failures) rating keeps the LPR/ANPR unit running for years before a service call. IP65-rated for outdoor parking lanes. Operating range -20°C to +65°C covers everything from a Nordic winter to a Gulf summer.

#### [Palm Vein + Iris Recognition](/products/palm-vein-iris/)

Palm vein reads the vein pattern under the skin — nothing to photograph, nothing to spoof. No facial image gets stored, which matters for sites where that's a legal concern. FAR below 0.0001% and FRR below 0.01% on the ESFPR. Iris recognition pushes accuracy further still: a 1-in-10,000,000 misrecognition rate, the highest of any biometric mode EASCO manufactures.

#### [Alcohol Detection Devices](/products/alcohol-detection/)

A worker can't clock in without passing a breath test first. The ESFAT combines alcohol detection with face recognition in one motion at the door. Testing range covers 0–400 mg/100mL at ±6.0 mg/100mL accuracy. Built for construction sites, logistics yards, and mining operations where a sober-only policy needs to be enforced at the door, not on the honor system.

#### [Visitor Registration Machines](/products/visitor-registration/)

Visitors log their own details, get their ID captured, and walk in with a printed badge. No front-desk staff member re-types a name into a spreadsheet. These terminals sit at the entrance of the same buildings that use EASCO's turnstile and biometric hardware. The visitor record and the staff access log can run on one platform.

### SECTION 3 — How to Choose the Right System

**H2: Traffic Volume and Environment Decide the Category — Not Preference**

A facility manager choosing between ten categories usually already knows two things. How many people cross the entrance per minute, and whether the installation sits indoors or exposed to weather. Those two answers eliminate most of the list before a spec table gets opened. A 300-seat auditorium exit needs the 45-person-per-minute cylindrical gate. A staff-only side door rarely needs more than a tripod turnstile.

Security tier narrows the rest. A palm vein or iris terminal earns its place where a facial image can't be stored for legal reasons. Or where a 1-in-10,000,000 false acceptance rate justifies the cost over standard face recognition. A parking structure needs ANPR, not a turnstile. An outdoor loading dock or an entrance without weather cover needs an IP65-rated terminal, regardless of which biometric mode gets chosen.

Industry context changes the combination, not the individual category. [Solutions by Industry](/solutions/) breaks down which categories pair together for commercial buildings, campuses, transit hubs, hospitals, factories, and government sites. Useful reading before a system integrator finalizes a bill of materials for a multi-entrance project.

**Q: What's the difference between a flap barrier and a cylindrical turnstile?**
A: A flap barrier uses swinging panels and suits standard lobby entrances. A cylindrical gate like the ESYBZ processes 45 people per minute through a rotating drum body, built for higher-traffic exits such as auditoriums or transit platforms.

**Q: Can these products work outdoors?**
A: IP65-rated models are available across face recognition, palm vein, iris, and ANPR categories — confirm the specific model's IP rating on its product page before specifying it for an uncovered installation.
