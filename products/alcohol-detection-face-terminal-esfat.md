## SECTION 8: HOW IT WORKS

**Eyebrow (red, uppercase):** HOW IT WORKS

**H2:** 4 Steps. From Scan to Access Decision.

Your ESFAT verifies identity and sobriety before allowing access:

**Step 1 — Face Recognition Scan**  
Employee approaches the ESFAT. The display prompts "Look at camera." The IR and RGB cameras capture the face, and the recognition engine compares against the 50,000-face employee database in 200–300ms. The employee name appears on screen.

**Step 2 — Breathalyzer Test**  
Display now prompts "Blow into sensor." Employee provides a breath sample into the port. The electrochemical sensor measures BAC and returns a numeric result (e.g., "0.02 mg/L"). The sensor provides haptic feedback (vibration) when sufficient breath volume is captured.

**Step 3 — Result Evaluation**  
The terminal compares the result against your facility's threshold policy. If BAC is below the limit (e.g., < 0.05 mg/L), the display shows "PASSED" in green. If BAC exceeds the limit, the display shows "FAILED — DENIED" in red and alerts management.

**Step 4 — Access Control Output**  
If the test passed, the ESFAT sends a relay signal to your door strike or barrier gate, unlocking entry. If failed, the door remains locked and a supervisor is alerted. The entire event (employee name, photo, BAC reading, timestamp, pass/fail status) is logged to your attendance system and compliance database.

## SECTION 9: FAQ

**Eyebrow (red, uppercase):** COMMON QUESTIONS

**H2:** What People Ask Before Installing ESFAT

**Q1: How often do we need to calibrate the breathalyzer sensor?**  
A1: Electrochemical sensors drift slightly over time. We recommend factory calibration every 6–12 months (per local regulations). EASCO provides calibration kits and services — contact us for scheduling.

**Q2: What's the difference between 0–400 mg/100mL and other BAC scales?**  
A2: Different countries use different units. 400 mg/100mL (milligrams per 100mL blood) is the European legal limit scale. Some countries use 0–2.0 mg/L, which is equivalent. The ESFAT display can be configured to show either unit.

**Q3: Can we set different BAC thresholds for different jobs?**  
A3: Yes. The terminal supports multiple policies. For example, "Drivers: < 0.01 mg/L, everyone else: < 0.05 mg/L." Your access control system can apply different threshold rules per employee role.

**Q4: What happens if an employee refuses to blow?**  
A4: A refusal is logged the same way as a failed test — access denied and supervisor alerted. Refusals can be tracked separately for HR discipline or legal proceedings. Your policy determines the consequence.

**Q5: Is the test result legally admissible in court?**  
A5: Electrochemical sensor readings from certified devices are generally admissible if the device was properly maintained and calibrated. ESFAT meets professional-grade standards. Consult your legal counsel about admissibility in your jurisdiction.

**Q6: What lead time for ESFAT delivery?**
