### SECTION 3: Key Features

**H2: Smaller Footprint, Same Safety Standard**

- **The arm retracts smoothly instead of jamming mid-swing.** Single-arm design built from an integrated aluminum alloy mold, not welded or bolted panels.
- **Nobody slips through on someone else's entry.** Infrared anti-tailgating sensor array flags a second person following through the same rotation.
- **A trapped hand doesn't happen twice.** Automatic anti-pinch detection stops the arm before contact, with automatic fault detection and voice broadcast alerts.
- **The gate still opens during a power cut.** Fail-safe design releases the arm on power loss, so an entrance never becomes a bottleneck during an outage.
- **One gate, three ways in.** Card reading, remote-controlled entry, or an add-on face recognition terminal, chosen per project without changing the gate body.

### SECTION 5: Applications

**H2: Where a Compact Gate Beats a Full Speed Gate**

A speed gate makes sense for a high-traffic lobby. A tripod turnstile makes more sense where the entrance is narrower, the budget is tighter, or the retrofit space is limited.

- **Factories and warehouses:** Worker entry points where a compact footprint matters more than peak throughput.
- **Schools:** Student entry monitoring at side and staff entrances, paired with card or remote access.
- **Gyms and leisure facilities:** Member check-in points where card access is the primary method.
- **Office buildings:** Lower-traffic zones and secondary entrances that don't justify a full speed gate.

### SECTION 8: FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: What's the difference between a tripod turnstile and a speed gate?**
A: A tripod turnstile uses one rotating arm and suits lower-traffic, budget-conscious entrances. A speed gate like the JX processes more people per minute through swinging glass panels, built for higher-traffic lobbies.

**Q: Is the tripod turnstile safe around children or the elderly?**
A: Automatic anti-pinch detection stops the arm before contact, and the gate releases automatically during a power cut, so it doesn't trap anyone in place.

**Q: Can someone follow another person through without scanning their own credential?**
A: An infrared anti-tailgating sensor array detects a second person following through the same rotation and flags the attempt.

**Q: Can we add face recognition later instead of ordering it now?**
A: Yes. The gate body accepts a face recognition terminal add-on, so card or remote access can be the starting configuration and biometrics added later.
