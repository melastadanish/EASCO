### SECTION 5 — Feature Deep-Dive Blocks

**H2: 850nm Infrared — Industry-Standard Wavelength, Financial-Grade Performance**

The 850nm wavelength was chosen for face recognition because it penetrates the skin surface to reveal subsurface vein patterns and bone structure that photographs cannot replicate. This makes 850nm infrared resistant to spoofing attempts (photos, masks, screens) — unlike visible-light cameras that can be fooled. The dual-array panel distributes infrared LEDs across a 120° cone, so recognition works regardless of whether the face approaches from directly in front, at an angle, or with slight head tilt. Individual LEDs are rated for 50,000+ hours continuous operation (5+ years at full brightness), and the panel is designed for passive thermal management — no cooling fan, no noise.

**H2: IP65 Weatherproof Housing — Deploy Indoors or Outdoors**

The 850nm panel is housed in a sealed aluminum enclosure rated IP65 — meaning it resists dust ingress and withstands direct water spray, washdown cycles, and coastal salt fog without performance degradation. Cable entry points use marine-grade compression fittings and potted connectors to prevent moisture from reaching the LED array. The mounting bracket includes quick-release hinges, so installers can pivot the panel to optimize beam angle without removing fasteners — critical for outdoor installations where sun angle changes seasonally. Stainless steel fasteners eliminate corrosion concerns on the panel frame.

### SECTION 12 — FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: Will the 850nm infrared interfere with regular security cameras?**
A: No. 850nm infrared is invisible to human eyes and standard RGB camera sensors. It only affects thermal imaging cameras. EASCO's Professional IR Kit is designed specifically for face recognition terminals and poses no interference risk to other security systems.

**Q: Can I use the IR kit outdoors in direct sunlight?**
A: Yes. The kit operates in direct sunlight, though performance is optimal in shaded outdoor areas (awnings, covered entries, building overhangs). Direct sunlight reduces the effective illumination distance because the 850nm LEDs are invisible to human observers and don't add visible brightness. Professional IR works best in environments with minimal ambient infrared (basements, garages, indoors).

**Q: How many brightness levels does the kit support?**
A: Six adjustable brightness levels, configured via the EASCO terminal's control panel. Level 1 is the minimum (conserves power), Level 6 is maximum (extends recognition distance but increases heat output and power consumption). Most installations use Level 3–4 for balanced performance.

**Q: Does the IR lighting kit work with EASCO terminals only?**
A: The kit is optimized for EASCO face recognition terminals with 850nm-compatible optics. For non-EASCO terminals, confirm that the terminal's camera sensor responds to 850nm wavelength and supports external infrared lighting before ordering. EASCO can customize connector types upon request.

**Q: What happens after 50,000 hours — can I replace just the LEDs?**
A: The LED panel is designed as a replaceable assembly — you don't need to purchase an entire new kit. Spare panels are available from EASCO and ship internationally within 10 business days. Replacement cost is approximately 40% of the full kit price.

**Q: Will weather or dust reduce the panel's performance over time?**
A: The IP65 housing prevents dust and water ingress, but accumulated dust on the lens cover will reduce light output. Annual cleaning (compressed air and soft brush) restores performance. In high-dust environments (construction sites, industrial floors), we recommend cleaning every 6 months.
