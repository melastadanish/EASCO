## Section 4 — What Is an Automatic Gate Opener?

**H2: What Is an Automatic Gate Opener?**

An automatic gate opener is an electric motor assembly that moves a gate or door on a set trigger — a remote control press, a card reader signal, a vehicle sensor, or a timed schedule. The motor connects to the gate via a floor spring, a gear rail, or a direct arm mount, depending on the gate type. When the signal arrives, the opener moves the gate, holds it open for the set dwell time, and then closes it again. No manual operation needed.

EASCO manufactures five gate opener models spanning single-swing residential doors up to 6-metre industrial gates. The floor spring controller handles light residential and commercial doors. The gear-and-slide-rail opener manages heavy sliding gates up to 1,000kg. The heavy-duty servo handles the largest commercial and industrial installations — 1,100kg gate weight, 6-metre span, 1.2KW continuous power. All models run on 24VDC or 24–36VDC motor voltage, keeping the powered mechanism at low voltage even where the input is 220VAC mains. All models include collision avoidance: the drive stops the instant it detects resistance, protecting anyone caught in the gate's path.

## Section 5 — Key Features

**H2: Key Features of EASCO Gate Openers**

**24VDC motor voltage eliminates electrical hazard at the gate.** The input may be 220VAC mains, but the motor that moves the gate runs on 24VDC. Anyone who contacts the gate mechanism during operation is not exposed to mains voltage. This matters in wet outdoor environments and in car parks where gates get touched constantly.

**5 million cycle motor lifespan on the heavy-duty model.** At a busy car park entry processing 200 vehicles per day, 5 million cycles equals 68 years of operation. The permanent magnet synchronous motor with Hall sensor control doesn't wear the way a brush motor does. Service intervals are long; replacements are rare.

**Absolute encoder positioning replaces manual limit switch calibration.** Traditional gate openers use physical limit switches to know when the gate has fully opened or closed. EASCO's encoder reads motor position continuously — no switch to drift, no manual recalibration when the gate's balance changes after installation.

**Soft start and stop protects the gate structure.** The drive ramps torque up from zero rather than slamming the gate into motion. Heavy gates that start and stop hard develop structural fatigue at the hinge or rail mount. Ramped acceleration and deceleration extends the gate frame's service life alongside the motor's.

## Section 8 — Frequently Asked Questions

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: What gate weight and width can EASCO gate openers handle?**
A: The range covers from lightweight single-swing doors (floor spring model, up to 1.2m) up to 6-metre gates weighing 1,100kg (heavy-duty model, BB-DD-01). The gear-and-slide-rail model handles sliding gates from 80kg to 1,000kg.

**Q: What is the operating temperature range?**
A: The gear-and-slide-rail model (BB-WSCLCT-01) covers the widest range at -40°C to +70°C. The floor spring and heavy-duty models operate from -25°C to +70°C. All models are suitable for outdoor installation.

**Q: How long does the heavy-duty motor last?**
A: The BB-DD-01 heavy-duty motor is rated for 5 million cycles. At 200 gate operations per day, that is approximately 68 years of continuous service life.

**Q: What control interfaces do the gate openers support?**
A: Standard interfaces include IR remote control, hard-wired access control system signals, RS485, and proximity sensor inputs. The WeChat Mini Program integration enables schedule-based automation, fault reporting, and remote firmware updates from a smartphone.

**Q: Is the gate safe if someone walks into it while it's closing?**
A: Yes — collision avoidance stops the drive immediately when resistance is detected. The motor stops before the gate can exert sustained force on a person or object in its path.

**Q: Can the gate opener integrate with a face recognition terminal?**
A: Yes — all models accept hard-wired relay signals from access control systems, including EASCO face recognition terminals. Wiring connects the terminal's output relay directly to the gate opener's trigger input.
