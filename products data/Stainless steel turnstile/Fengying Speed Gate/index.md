## 7. How It Works

**Eyebrow:** HOW IT WORKS

**H2:** Credential In. Gate Open. 4 Steps.

**Subtitle:** Every Fengying gate follows the same control flow — from credential check to passage — in under a second.

**Step 1 — Identity Verification**
The add-on credential module — card, QR code, or face recognition panel — processes the authorization. External button or remote control operates the gate without a module fitted.

**Step 2 — Controller Signal**
The ARM control core sends an open signal via RS485, RS232, or IO relay. Wiegand 26/34 output is available for third-party access control integration.

**Step 3 — Gate Movement**
The brushless motor opens the gate in under 0.5 seconds. Six infrared pairs monitor the passage throughout. Throughput reaches 50 persons per minute.

**Step 4 — Safety & Reset**
Resistance triggers reverse braking and three-stage torque compensation. On power cut, the gate opens and the ARM core retains position — no recalibration on restart.

## 8. FAQ

**Eyebrow:** COMMON QUESTIONS

**H2:** What Buyers Ask Before Ordering the Fengying Stainless Steel Speed Gate

**Q1:** Why choose stainless steel over aluminum alloy for a speed gate?
**A1:** SUS304 stainless steel is more resistant to corrosion from cleaning chemicals, moisture, and humid environments. It suits hospitals, food production facilities, and institutional buildings where the gate is wiped down regularly with strong cleaning agents. Aluminum alloy is lighter and better suited to aesthetics-focused premium lobbies.

**Q2:** What throughput does the Fengying deliver?
**A2:** 50 persons per minute in continuous flow. The gate opens in under 0.5 seconds. Passage width is configurable from 550 mm to 1,200 mm.

**Q3:** What credential modules can be added?

**Q4:** Will it integrate with our access control system?
**A4:** RS485, RS232, and IO relay interfaces connect to building management and access control platforms. Cloud platform and WeChat mini-program are included free.

**Q5:** Does it have anti-crush protection?
**A5:** Yes — 6 infrared pairs are standard. Reverse braking and three-stage torque compensation engage on resistance detection before contact is made.
