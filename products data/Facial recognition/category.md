## Section 4 — What Is a Face Recognition Terminal?

**H2: What Is a Face Recognition Terminal?**

A face recognition terminal is a wall-mounted or desk-mounted biometric device that grants or denies access based on a live facial scan. The terminal captures the face via an IR camera and an RGB camera, compares the scan against an onboard enrollment database, and either opens the connected door or gate — or holds it shut — in a single pass. The whole process takes 200–300 milliseconds: faster than a card tap.

EASCO manufactures face recognition terminals across three display sizes and two camera tiers. Compact 7-inch models suit tight lobbies and narrow corridors. Standard 8-inch models handle general-purpose access control for offices, campuses, and factories. The premium 10.1-inch model with an 8MP camera and Android 13 handles the highest-volume entry points and integrates easily with third-party software via SDK. All models share the same core specification: 99.98% accuracy, 50,000-face onboard storage (10,000 on the ESBL), IP65 rating, and financial-grade liveness detection. For sites that need a higher biometric bar — healthcare, government, financial — the ESQV8 adds palm vein recognition and the MTH8 adds iris recognition on top of face.

## Section 5 — Key Features

**H2: Key Features of EASCO Face Recognition Terminals**

**A photo cannot open the door.** Financial-grade liveness detection uses multi-spectral imaging to detect blood flow and skin texture in a live face. Photographs, videos played on a screen, silicone masks, and 3D-printed faces all fail the check. This is not consumer-grade software — it is the same liveness standard used in financial sector authentication.

**Recognition at 0.5 to 3 metres — no stopping at the reader.** Most face terminals require the person to stand still, close up. EASCO terminals recognize a face from half a metre to 3 metres away, at walking speed. In a high-traffic corridor or turnstile lane, staff move through without breaking stride.

**50,000 faces stored onboard. No server required for access decisions.** The enrollment database lives on the terminal itself. Basic access control runs with no network dependency — useful in sites with unreliable connectivity or strict data residency rules. The terminal still syncs attendance records and enrollment updates via Ethernet, WiFi, or RS485 when the network is available.

**IP65 means it works on the building exterior.** Dust cannot enter the housing. Water jets cannot damage it. The operating temperature range of -25°C to +75°C covers tropical heat and northern winters. Mount it at a covered outdoor entrance, on an exterior wall, or on a turnstile exposed to the elements.

## Section 8 — Frequently Asked Questions

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: What is the accuracy of EASCO face recognition terminals?**
A: EASCO face recognition terminals achieve 99.98% accuracy at 200–300ms recognition speed, with a recognition distance of 0.5 to 3 metres. Liveness detection is financial-grade, rejecting photographs, video replays, and silicone masks.

**Q: Can the terminal be fooled by a photo or a mask?**
A: No — financial-grade liveness detection uses multi-spectral imaging to verify a live face. It distinguishes real skin texture and blood flow from photographs, screen-displayed videos, silicone masks, and 3D-printed facial replicas.

**Q: How many faces can the terminal store onboard?**
A: Standard and premium models store 50,000 faces onboard. The compact ESBL stores 10,000. Local storage means basic access control runs without a network connection.

**Q: Is the terminal suitable for outdoor installation?**
A: Yes — all models are IP65 rated (dust-proof and water jet resistant) and operate from -25°C to +75°C. They install on exterior walls, covered outdoor entrances, and directly onto turnstile gate housings.

**Q: What access methods does the terminal support besides face recognition?**
A: All models support card access via Wiegand 26/34. Most models also support QR code scanning and PIN. The ESQV8 adds palm vein recognition. The MTH8 adds iris recognition. Multi-modal combinations — face + card, face + PIN — are configurable.

**Q: How does the terminal connect to my existing access control system?**
A: EASCO face recognition terminals connect via Ethernet, WiFi 2.4GHz, RS485, Wiegand 26/34, and USB 2.0. SDK and API documentation is available for third-party software integration.
