## Section 4 — What Is a Face Recognition Terminal?

**H2: What Is a Face Recognition Terminal?**

A face recognition terminal is a wall-mounted or desk-mounted biometric device that grants or denies access based on a live facial scan matched against an onboard enrollment database. The terminal captures the face via an infrared camera and an RGB wide-dynamic camera, runs the comparison locally, and either signals the connected door or gate to open — or holds it shut — in a single pass. The whole process takes 200–300 milliseconds.

EASCO manufactures face recognition terminals in four display sizes. The 8-inch format is the most widely deployed: compact enough for a single door frame, with 50,000-face onboard storage and IP65 rating for indoor and outdoor use. The ESJ model is the confirmed 8-inch product with full spec documentation. The 10-inch QF10 adds a larger display for higher-footfall environments. The 15.6-inch ES-RCM03 and 21.5-inch ES-RCM04 are large-format terminals designed for lobby kiosk and attendance station deployments — both support landscape and portrait orientation, advertising video playback, and 12-language interfaces. All models share the same recognition core: 99.98% accuracy, 0.5–3 metre recognition distance, IP65 housing, operating range -25°C to +75°C.

## Section 8 — Frequently Asked Questions

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: What is the recognition accuracy of EASCO face recognition terminals?**
A: All EASCO face recognition terminals achieve 99.98% accuracy at 200–300ms recognition speed. Recognition distance is 0.5 to 3 metres. Financial-grade liveness detection is active on all models.

**Q: Can the terminal be fooled by a photo or a mask?**
A: No — financial-grade liveness detection uses multi-spectral imaging to verify a live face. It distinguishes real skin texture and blood flow from photographs, screen-displayed videos, silicone masks, and 3D-printed facial replicas.

**Q: How many faces can a terminal store?**
A: All confirmed EASCO face recognition terminal models store 50,000 faces onboard. Local storage means access decisions run without a network connection.

**Q: Is the terminal suitable for outdoor installation?**
A: The 8-inch models (including the ESJ) are IP65-rated and operate from -25°C to +75°C — suitable for outdoor walls, covered entrances, and turnstile gate integration. The 15.6-inch and 21.5-inch large-screen models are rated for indoor use.

**Q: What access methods does the terminal support?**
A: Face recognition is standard. Card access via Wiegand 26/34 is supported on all models. QR code reading is supported on confirmed models including the ESJ, ES-RCM03, and ES-RCM04. Contact EASCO to confirm multi-modal options for a specific model.

**Q: How does the terminal connect to an existing access control system?**
A: EASCO face recognition terminals connect via Ethernet, WiFi 2.4GHz, RS-485, Wiegand 26/34, and USB 2.0. SDK and API documentation is available for third-party software integration.
