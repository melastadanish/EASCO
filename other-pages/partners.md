### SECTION 2: Partnership Types

**H2: Three Ways to Work With EASCO**

**Distributor / Reseller**
Stock and resell EASCO's turnstile and biometric access control range under your own storefront or sales team, at factory-direct pricing.

**OEM / ODM Manufacturing**
Private-label EASCO hardware under your own brand, with custom housing, logo, and packaging. See the [OEM/ODM page](/oem-odm/) for details.

**System Integrator Partner**
Get SDK/API documentation, technical support, and priority quote turnaround for projects that combine EASCO hardware with third-party access control software.

### SECTION 3: Why Partner With EASCO

**H2: What a Partner Gets That an Individual Order Doesn't**

- **Territory consideration.** A limited number of partners per region avoids one distributor undercutting another on the same product line.
- **Factory-direct pricing.** No reseller markup between the production line and the partner's cost basis.
- **Direct access to R&D.** Product and customization questions reach the same engineers who built the hardware, not a call center reading from a script.
- **16+ years of manufacturing history.** A partner inherits a track record already built, rather than launching a new brand from zero.

### SECTION 5: FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: Is there a minimum order commitment to become a partner?**
A: Minimum volume expectations vary by territory and partnership type. Discuss specifics directly with a sales specialist.

**Q: Do you limit the number of partners in one territory?**
A: Yes. Territory exclusivity or limited partner slots are discussed and confirmed during onboarding, not guaranteed by default.

**Q: Can a system integrator become a partner without reselling hardware?**
A: Yes. A system integrator partnership focuses on technical support and priority quote turnaround rather than resale volume.

**Q: What's the difference between a reseller and an OEM partner?**
A: A reseller sells EASCO-branded product. An OEM partner private-labels the hardware under its own brand. See the [OEM/ODM page](/oem-odm/) for OEM specifics.

**Q: How long does onboarding take?**
A: Onboarding timelines depend on the partnership type and territory discussion. Most initial terms are confirmed within a few business days of first contact.
