## S6 — FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

---

**Q: How accurate is number plate recognition in low light, rain, or at night?**
A: The ES-T02/T03/T04 cameras include night vision and are rated for partial plate occlusion — the system identifies plates where part of the plate is obscured by dirt, glare, or angle. IP65 certification covers dust and water-jet exposure. For recognition accuracy in specific weather conditions at your site, send your location and the technical team will confirm the right camera model and mounting position.

---

**Q: What is the lifespan of an EASCO LPR system?**
A: The number plate recognition system is rated for an average lifespan of 50,000 hours of continuous operation, with an MTBF (mean time between failures) of 30,000 hours. Both figures are confirmed in the ES-T02/T03/T04 specification. For sites requiring 24/7 operation — logistics yards, hospitals, gated communities — this covers over five years of uninterrupted use.

---

**Q: Can the barrier gate integrate with existing access control or site management software?**
A: The number plate recognition system communicates via RS-485 transparent transmission — compatible with most site management platforms. SDK and API documentation are available for custom integration. For Wiegand-based systems already installed on site, confirm compatibility requirements when requesting a quote.

---

**Q: Does the camera system work at high vehicle speeds?**
A: Recognition speed is rated in milliseconds — fast enough to capture plates at normal site entry speeds without requiring vehicles to stop at the camera. For exact recognition speed figures and the maximum vehicle speed your site configuration supports, request the ES-T02/T03/T04 specification sheet.

---

**Q: Can one system manage both entry and exit lanes?**
A: Yes — the number plate recognition system logs both entry and exit events, with timestamps against each plate. For multi-lane sites, the technical team will specify the number of cameras and barrier gates required based on your peak vehicle volume and lane configuration.
