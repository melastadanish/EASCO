## S6 — FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

---

**Q: What is the difference between face recognition, palm vein, and iris recognition?**
A: Face recognition identifies at 0.5–3 metres — suited to high-throughput, hands-free entry at 200–300ms per person. Palm vein reads the subsurface vein pattern beneath the skin using near-infrared light; no face data is collected or stored, making it the right choice where facial recognition raises privacy concerns. Iris recognition delivers the highest accuracy of the three: a misrecognition rate of 1 in 10,000,000.

---

**Q: Can EASCO biometric systems be fooled by a photo, video, or mask?**
A: Liveness detection — built to financial-sector standards — distinguishes a live face from a photo, screen replay, silicone mask, or 3D-printed replica. It is not consumer-grade face unlock. For palm vein, the ESFPR sensor reads subsurface infrared patterns that no photograph or surface replication can reproduce.

---

**Q: What false acceptance rate do EASCO biometric terminals achieve?**
A: Face recognition terminals achieve 99.98% accuracy. The palm vein system (ESFPR) delivers a FAR below 0.0001% — fewer than 1 in 1,000,000 access attempts by an unauthorized person results in entry. Iris recognition reaches the highest level: 1 in 10,000,000.

---

**Q: Do EASCO biometric terminals work outdoors?**
A: All terminals carry IP65 certification — dust-tight and water-jet resistant. The operating temperature range is -25°C to +75°C, covering tropical climates, cold-weather installations, and industrial environments. IR illumination enables face recognition at 0.5–3 metres in any lighting condition, including complete darkness and direct sunlight.

---

**Q: What control systems do EASCO biometric terminals connect to?**
A: Standard interfaces are Ethernet, WiFi 2.4GHz, RS-485, Wiegand 26/34, and USB 2.0. For sites running existing Wiegand infrastructure, the terminal connects directly — no controller replacement required. SDK and API documentation are available for custom integration with building management or HR systems.
