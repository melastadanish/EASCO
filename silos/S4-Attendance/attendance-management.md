## S6 — FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

---

**Q: Can employees clock in for each other?**
A: No. The terminal requires the enrolled face to be present — a photo, video replay, or another person's face does not pass liveness detection. Buddy punching is structurally impossible because the biometric template cannot be delegated. Recognition accuracy is 99.98% across 50,000 enrolled faces.

---

**Q: What happens if an employee fails the alcohol test?**
A: The ESFAT terminal denies clock-in and logs the failed attempt with a timestamp against the employee record. The alcohol test range covers 0–400 mg/100mL with an accuracy of ±6.0 mg/100mL. For sites where the logged record needs to feed a disciplinary or safety compliance system, SDK integration is available on request.

---

**Q: How many employees can the system store?**
A: The standard terminal stores 50,000 enrolled faces onboard — no cloud dependency required for recognition. For sites with more than 50,000 employees or multiple locations sharing a single enrollment database, the technical team will specify the right network configuration.

---

**Q: Does the attendance terminal work outdoors on construction sites?**
A: The ESFAT terminal carries IP65 certification — dust-tight and water-jet resistant — with an operating temperature range of -25°C to +75°C. For handheld terminals used across outdoor zones, confirm the IP rating and battery specification with the technical team before ordering.

---

**Q: What payroll or HR systems does the attendance terminal connect to?**
A: Standard interfaces are Ethernet, WiFi 2.4GHz, RS-485, Wiegand 26/34, and USB 2.0. SDK and API documentation are available for custom integration with payroll platforms, HR systems, and site management software. For sites already running a Wiegand-based access control system, the attendance terminal connects to the same infrastructure.
