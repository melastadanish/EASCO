## Section 4 — What Is a Face Recognition Terminal?

**H2: What Is a Face Recognition Terminal?**

A face recognition terminal is a biometric access control device that identifies people by scanning their facial features and comparing them against an enrolled database. EASCO face terminals run a full Android operating system onboard — no separate access control server required for basic operation. Each unit stores up to 50,000 face templates, recognizes enrolled faces in 200–300 milliseconds, and achieves 99.98% accuracy. Financial-grade liveness detection rejects photographs, videos, silicone masks, and 3D-printed faces — technology originally designed for banking and government use. Installation options include wall-mounted, desktop, floor-standing, or turnstile-integrated. Facility managers use face recognition where badge checking creates bottlenecks, where badge-and-PIN fraud is a problem, or where regulatory compliance (HIPAA, financial services) mandates biometric verification.

## Section 5 — Key Features of Face Recognition Terminals

**H2: Key Features of Face Recognition Terminals**

**99.98% accuracy eliminates false accepts and false rejects.** That level of accuracy comes from 2–8MP infrared and RGB cameras working together. The infrared channel works in complete darkness. The RGB channel provides color and fine detail. Fusion of both channels delivers accuracy no single-camera system can match.

**Financial-grade liveness detection prevents spoofing with photographs or masks.** Consumer face recognition (phone unlock) rejects photos about 70% of the time. EASCO terminals use multi-modal liveness testing that rejects photos, screen replays, silicone masks, and 3D-printed faces 99.9% of the time. Technology originally built for banking and border control — not smartphone convenience.

**Recognition works in low light, sunlight, and darkness.** IR illumination enables face recognition without visible light. Outdoor terminals see faces in direct sun glare, shadows, and night conditions. No backlighting needed. No special installation angles required.

**Onboard Android OS means minimal integration work.** Full operating system runs on the terminal itself. Face templates store locally. Access decisions happen locally. Cloud sync and remote management are optional, not required. One network cable. One power line. That's it.

## Section 8 — Frequently Asked Questions

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How accurate is face recognition really — can it be fooled by a photo?**
A: EASCO terminals achieve 99.98% accuracy and use financial-grade liveness detection that rejects photographs, videos, silicone masks, and 3D-printed faces. Consumer face recognition (smartphone unlock) rejects photos ~70% of the time. Banking-grade rejection is 99.9%+.

**Q: How fast does facial recognition work?**
A: EASCO terminals recognize enrolled faces in 200–300 milliseconds — fast enough for pedestrian throughput at office and campus gates without bottleneck. Speed doesn't depend on lighting; IR illumination enables recognition in complete darkness.

**Q: Can face recognition work outdoors in sunlight?**
A: Yes — dual IR and RGB cameras allow recognition in direct sun, shadows, backlit conditions, and complete darkness. No special lighting or angles required. IP65-rated models are designed for outdoor installation.

**Q: What happens if someone's face changes — beard, glasses, weight loss?**
A: EASCO terminals are trained on variation. Growing a beard or wearing glasses typically does not trigger false rejection. If genuine facial changes occur (surgery, aging), re-enrollment takes seconds. Multi-spectral analysis means changes to appearance (makeup, lighting) don't disrupt recognition.

**Q: How many faces can a terminal store?**
A: Standard models store 50,000 faces locally on the device. Premium models with larger storage can exceed that. For larger enterprise deployments, cloud sync options enable central enrollment management.

**Q: How does face recognition integrate with my existing access control system?**
A: EASCO terminals support Wiegand, RS485, TCP/IP, and Ethernet interfaces. Door locks, turnstiles, and gate openers integrate via standard protocols. Cloud API available for enterprise security management systems.
