## SECTION 3: Challenges in Factories & Warehouses

**H2: What Access Control Has to Solve in Factories & Warehouses**

**Sober-Only Policy Enforcement**

Construction sites, logistics hubs, and mining operations face safety liability if impaired workers operate machinery or work at height. Traditional clock-in systems do not verify sobriety. Manual breath-testing at each site entrance requires staffing and is inconsistent. EASCO combines face recognition with alcohol detection—every employee clock-in includes an automatic breathalyzer reading. Results are logged per person, per shift. If an employee tests above the configured limit, the system prevents clock-in and alerts supervisors. Legally defensible, automatically enforced, no manual testing.

**Multi-Shift Attendance Across Extended Hours**

Large facilities run 24/7: day shift, swing shift, night shift, plus weekend and holiday staffing. One central attendance terminal cannot serve 500+ employees clocking in during all hours. Multiple biometric terminals spread across the site allow employees to clock in at their work area entrance. Every terminal authenticates using the same enrolled face database, synced across the site. Time stamps and location (which gate) are logged per employee, giving supervisors visibility into shift coverage and site utilization.

**Site Spread and Distributed Access Control**

Warehouses and factory floors span acres. Office buildings, parking lots, dock areas, manufacturing floor, storage zones—each requires its own access control. Portable handheld biometric terminals enable quick enrollment at distributed locations without requiring employees to visit a central office. Supervisors can enroll new hires at the dock or on the manufacturing floor, then sync enrollment data to the central system via WiFi or USB. Eliminates bottleneck, accelerates onboarding, reduces administrative overhead.

## SECTION 8: FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How does alcohol detection plus face recognition work together?**
A: When an employee approaches the terminal, the system first scans their face (99.98% accuracy). If the face matches an enrolled employee, the system automatically triggers a breathalyzer scan. The employee breathes into the sensor. If the reading is below the facility's configured threshold (e.g., 0.05% BAC), the employee clocks in and the door unlocks. If above threshold, clock-in is denied and supervisors are alerted. Both face and alcohol results are logged.

**Q: Is the alcohol test result legally defensible?**
A: EASCO alcohol sensors test positive to ±6.0 mg/100mL accuracy. This is suitable for enforcing internal safety policies (preventing impaired workers from entering the facility). However, BAC results are not suitable for legal prosecution in most jurisdictions—they require certified laboratory testing. Use EASCO results for policy enforcement, not legal proceedings. Consult local regulations for your specific use case.

**Q: Can we have multiple clock-in terminals across our large facility?**
A: Yes. Multiple face recognition terminals across the site all authenticate against the same enrolled face database. Each terminal syncs to the central system, so supervisors can view real-time attendance from any gate. You can also use handheld portable terminals to enroll new hires on-site without a central office visit.

**Q: What if an employee's shift is at night? Does face recognition work in low light?**
A: Yes. All EASCO face recognition terminals use 2MP infrared cameras, enabling recognition in complete darkness. IR illumination lights the employee's face; the algorithm recognizes the thermal pattern. Works regardless of ambient light or time of day.

**Q: How do we manage exceptions like a sick day or an exempt early departure?**
A: System administrators can manually log clock-out times, approve exceptions, or mark absences in the attendance records. The biometric terminal provides the base log; supervisors override as needed. All overrides are logged with timestamp and approver name for audit compliance.
