## SECTION 3: Challenges in Transportation Hubs

**H2: What Access Control Has to Solve in Transportation Hubs**

**Peak-Hour Passenger Throughput**

Airport security checkpoints process thousands of passengers in 2–3 hour windows during morning departure peaks. Metro systems see 20,000+ passengers per hour during evening rush. Any delay at the entry gate creates a bottleneck: passengers miss flights, platform crowding escalates, security supervision becomes reactive instead of preventive. A single slow gate that processes 20 people per minute costs hours in aggregate delay per day. EASCO speed gates process 50 persons per minute (standard models) — the Interstellar Series exceeds 50/min with <0.3-second opening speed — eliminating the single-point bottleneck.

**24/7 Continuous Operation Without Downtime**

Airports and metros never close. 3am emergency exits must work. 2am maintenance windows cannot exceed 30 minutes. Hardware failures trigger cascading delays. EASCO gates are mechanical-first with servo control backup—crank-link transmission and magnetic-sensitive limit switches stop the gate regardless of software state. No power loss, no gate malfunction. 80,000+ installations worldwide prove the hardware does not fail in the real world.

**Passenger Flow Logging & Compliance**

Regulatory authorities (FAA, national transport agencies) require audit trails: exactly who passed each gate, at what time. Electronic logs must be tamper-proof and retained for months or years. A gate that fails once per 10,000 passengers creates compliance gaps—a single unlogged passenger during a security investigation weakens the entire audit trail. EASCO speed gates integrate with standard access control systems (Wiegand, RS485, TCP/IP) and log every transit—with or without biometric data. Camera integration captures facial images for passenger-level audit trails if required.

## SECTION 8: FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How do EASCO gates handle 50 people per minute without jamming or security failures?**
A: Speed gates move on servo-controlled rails with constant acceleration and deceleration. Each transit is metered—the gate opens (<0.5s standard, <0.3s for Interstellar), a person passes, the gate re-closes, and repeats. No stalling, no manual intervention needed. Tested under peak load in real airport and metro environments, not just lab conditions.

**Q: What happens if the power fails during peak travel?**
A: The gate defaults to safe fail-open—passengers can walk through without being trapped. For security checkpoints, fail-safe configuration (closes until power returns) is also available. Crank-link transmission ensures the gate cannot jam or lock up. Manual override is always accessible.

**Q: Do EASCO gates integrate with existing airport/metro access control systems?**
A: Yes. Standard interfaces include Wiegand 26/34 (card readers), RS485, TCP/IP, USB, and WiFi. Integration with legacy Ethernet-based control systems is standard. If you use a proprietary or non-standard system, we provide SDK documentation for custom integration.

**Q: Can the gates log passenger data for regulatory compliance?**
A: Yes. Every transit can be logged with timestamp, gate number, and passenger identity (if biometric or card data is present). Integration with IP cameras captures facial images per passenger for audit trails. All logs can be exported for compliance reviews. Data retention is configurable.

**Q: What's the typical downtime for maintenance?**
A: Routine maintenance (cleaning, lubrication) takes 15–30 minutes and can be done during off-peak hours. No parts are typically consumed during maintenance. Major service or part replacement happens 1–2 times per year depending on traffic volume. EASCO provides 24/7 phone support for emergency issues.
