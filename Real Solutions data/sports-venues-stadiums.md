## SECTION 3: Challenges in Sports Venues & Stadiums

**H2: What Access Control Has to Solve in Sports Venues & Stadiums**

**High-Volume Spectator Throughput on Event Day**

A 60,000-seat stadium fills in 60–90 minutes before kickoff. That is 666–1000 spectators per minute through entry gates. A single slow gate that processes 20–25 people per minute creates cascading backup—spectators miss kickoff, parking lots overflow, exterior crowds become safety risks. EASCO speed gates process 50 people per minute per gate—more than double standard performance. Multiple gates operating simultaneously move the full crowd in under 90 minutes without crushing, bottleneck, or compromise to ticket verification.

**Staff Zone Separation During High Crowd Density**

Game-day crowds fill public areas: main concourse, seating areas, concessions. Simultaneous staff operations happen in restricted zones: broadcast booth, VIP lounges, player areas, security command center, medical facilities. Manual staff gate supervision becomes impossible when 1000 spectators per minute are passing nearby entrances. A biometric system with role-based access automatically enforces boundaries—staff badges never enter restricted zones, staff faces unlock staff-only doors—no manual verification needed. Spectator crowd does not interfere with staff operations.

**Event-Day Temporary Credential Management**

For a single game, 100–200 event-day personnel are added: temporary vendors, broadcast staff, corporate guests, medical contractors. Traditional badge systems require pre-printing, manual distribution, and end-of-day collection. Biometric enrollment is faster: handheld terminals enroll new staff on-site during pre-event setup. Temporary access is time-limited (ends at game-end timestamp). Credentials expire automatically—no manual badge collection needed. All entries are logged with identity and timestamp for liability and security.

## SECTION 6: FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How do 50-person-per-minute speed gates prevent crowding at entry during pre-game rush?**
A: A single gate moving 50 people/minute clears 3,000 spectators per hour. A stadium with 4 gates running in parallel clears 12,000 per hour. Most stadiums reach full capacity (60,000) in 90 minutes with 5–6 gates operating. Each gate opens in <0.5 seconds (standard) or <0.3 seconds (Interstellar); spectators pass through continuously without jams. No backup forms because the throughput rate matches the crowd arrival rate.

**Q: Can we integrate speed gates with ticket scanning systems?**
A: Yes. EASCO speed gates support integration with ticket readers, QR code scanners, and access control platforms via Wiegand, RS485, and TCP/IP. Spectators scan their ticket at the gate; the system validates the ticket and opens the gate in one motion. Multiple entry types (season pass, day ticket, corporate) are supported.

**Q: How do we prevent staff from sneaking through spectator gates?**
A: Role-based access control ensures staff face recognition only unlocks staff-only doors, not spectator entry gates. Staff cannot pass through spectator turnstiles even if they wanted to—those gates accept only valid spectator tickets. Entry path is determined by role (staff vs. spectator), not by individual choice.

**Q: Can we enroll temporary event staff (vendors, contractors) the morning of an event?**
A: Yes. Handheld biometric terminals enroll new staff on-site during pre-event setup (2–3 hours before gates open). Enrollment takes 30 seconds per person. Temporary credentials are time-limited: access expires at an assigned end time (e.g., 11pm after the event ends). No badge collection needed—credentials disappear automatically.

**Q: What happens if a temporary credential is issued to the wrong person by mistake?**
A: Security can revoke the credential immediately via central system. The person's face is removed from the allowlist. If they attempt access afterward, the system denies entry and alerts security. Complete audit trail shows who was enrolled, when, and every access attempt they made.
