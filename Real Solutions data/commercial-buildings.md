## SECTION 3: Challenges in Commercial Buildings

**H2: What Access Control Has to Solve in Commercial Buildings**

**Multi-Tenant Floor Separation**

Corporate buildings house dozens of companies across multiple floors. Employees from Tenant A should not access Tenant B's floor; vendors should only reach the designated tenant space. Traditional card readers cannot prevent someone sharing a stolen badge with a colleague. Biometric systems solve this by tying access to the individual—one face, one fingerprint, one enrolled person per credential.

**Visitor Flow Without Bottlenecks**

Every visitor arrives at the main lobby. Reception staff must verify identity, issue a temporary badge, note which floor/tenant they're visiting, and track when they leave. For 500-person buildings with 50+ daily visitors, bottlenecks create security gaps (tailgating through turnstiles) and guest frustration (15-minute wait at the gate). A speed gate processing 50 people per minute clears morning rush in minutes, not hours—with IP cameras and facial recognition logging every entry.

**Round-the-Clock Facility Access**

Office buildings operate 24/7. Janitors clean at night. Security patrols midnight shifts. Early arrivals work before 7am. Late teams work past 6pm. A single central credential system (turnstile or biometric terminal) at the main entry, plus door openers on emergency exits and back doors, ensures one system tracks all traffic regardless of time of day or entry point.

## SECTION 8: FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How many people per minute can EASCO turnstiles handle?**
A: The aluminum alloy speed gate range processes 50 persons per minute — fast enough to clear a 500-person building lobby in under 10 minutes during morning rush. The Interstellar Series exceeds 50/min with a <0.3-second opening. That throughput is measured in real installations, not lab conditions.

**Q: Can I integrate face recognition with my existing access control system?**
A: Yes. EASCO face recognition terminals support Wiegand 26/34, RS485, TCP/IP, USB, and WiFi—standard interfaces on most access control platforms. If your system uses a different protocol, we provide SDK documentation for custom integration.

**Q: What happens if someone tries to share a badge with a colleague?**
A: Biometric systems (face, palm vein, iris) prevent badge-sharing entirely. The person who enrolled cannot be swapped out—their face, palm pattern or iris must match the credential. This solves buddy punching and unauthorized access in ways cards alone cannot.

**Q: Do turnstiles and biometric terminals work in a building without a central access control system?**
A: Yes. All EASCO systems are standalone-capable. Each device can store and authenticate hundreds or thousands of enrolled users locally, without connecting to a central database. Larger buildings often connect them to a central system for real-time reporting; smaller buildings run them standalone.

**Q: What's the typical lead time for a commercial building system?**
A: Standard products: 15–25 business days production (confirm exact timeline with us). Custom configurations (multi-floor, large tenant count): 25–40 days. Once ordered, systems typically ship within 5–7 business days and arrive 2–3 weeks after shipment depending on destination. Expedited options available.
