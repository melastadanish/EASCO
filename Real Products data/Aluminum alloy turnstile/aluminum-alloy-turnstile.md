### SECTION 4 — What Is an Aluminum Alloy Turnstile?

**H2: What Is an Aluminum Alloy Turnstile?**

An aluminum alloy turnstile is a pedestrian access gate built on an aircraft-grade aluminum frame instead of stainless steel, giving facility managers a lighter, lower-cost gate without giving up the speed or durability needed for daily high-traffic use. EASCO's aluminum range covers cylindrical speed gates, straight-bar gates and compact mini gates, all built around the same core mechanism: a DC brushless servo motor driving glass or acrylic doors that open in as little as 0.3 seconds. Because the frame is aluminum rather than steel, these gates are faster to ship, easier to install, and typically priced lower per lane — which is why they're the default choice for corporate lobbies, factory entrances and university buildings, reserving stainless steel for sites with heavier corrosion or vandalism exposure. Buyers choosing between the two materials should weigh environment (indoor vs. coastal/outdoor) and budget against the stainless steel range's added corrosion resistance.

### SECTION 5 — Key Features of Aluminum Alloy Turnstiles

**H2: Key Features of Aluminum Alloy Turnstiles**

**Fast lane throughput without a flap-barrier footprint.** Every gate in EASCO's aluminum range is built around a DC brushless servo motor that opens the passage doors in as little as 0.3 seconds, giving a rated throughput of up to 45 persons per minute per lane. That speed comes from the same crank-link, magnetic-sensitive limit control mechanism used across EASCO's turnstile range, which also stops the door instantly if it meets resistance — so faster throughput doesn't come at the cost of pinch safety.

**Aircraft-grade frame, built for continuous daily cycling.** The frame on every aluminum model is aircraft-grade aluminum alloy, paired with 10mm acrylic or toughened-glass doors depending on the series. This keeps the gate light enough for straightforward installation and retrofits into existing lobbies, while still holding up to the thousands of open/close cycles a busy corporate or campus entrance generates each day.

**Built-in biometric and credential support.** Several models in the range — including the Android-based series — run a full onboard OS, meaning face, palm, card and QR code readers can be integrated directly into the gate without a separate controller box. For facility managers standardizing on one credential type across a site, this cuts down on the extra hardware an integrator would otherwise need to mount and wire.
