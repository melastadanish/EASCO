## Section 4 — What Is an Aluminum Alloy Turnstile?

**H2: What Is an Aluminum Alloy Turnstile?**

An aluminum alloy turnstile is a pedestrian access gate built on an aircraft-grade aluminum frame instead of stainless steel. You get the speed and durability you need without paying for corrosion resistance you don't. EASCO's aluminum range covers cylindrical speed gates and compact entry barriers — all built around a DC brushless servo motor that swings the doors open in 0.3 seconds. The frame is light enough for fast installation and retrofits. The doors are 10mm acrylic or toughened glass. Pick aluminum for indoor facilities where throughput and budget matter. Pick stainless steel only if you're installing outdoors or in high-corrosion zones.

---

## Section 5 — Key Features of Aluminum Alloy Turnstiles

**H2: Key Features of Aluminum Alloy Turnstiles**

**Fast enough for transit-grade entry without a flap barrier footprint.** Every EASCO aluminum gate opens in 0.3 seconds and processes up to 45 people per minute per lane. Speed comes from a DC brushless servo motor, not a weak solenoid. That same motor drives a crank-link transmission that stops instantly if it meets resistance — so speed doesn't mean pinch hazard.

**Aircraft-grade frame rated for thousands of open-close cycles per day.** Aluminum alloy doesn't rot, doesn't rust, doesn't need painting. It handles the weight of glass or acrylic doors plus years of daily cycling. Install it once. It stays working.

**Biometric and credential readers mount directly on the gate.** Several models in the aluminum range run a full Android operating system onboard. That means face, palm, card, and QR code access can integrate without a separate controller box bolted to the wall. Standard interfaces include Wiegand and RS485 for integration with third-party access control systems. One device. One power line. One network drop.

**Anti-tailgating detection built in.** Infrared sensor array detects when a second person tries to slip through behind an authorized entry. Gate re-closes before the second person passes. No tailgating. No unauthorized access.

## Section 8 — Frequently Asked Questions

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How fast do aluminum alloy turnstiles open?**
A: EASCO's aluminum alloy turnstiles open in 0.3 seconds, processing up to 45 people per minute per lane. Speed comes from the DC brushless servo motor and crank-link transmission — the same mechanism used in all EASCO turnstile ranges.

**Q: What's the difference between aluminum alloy and stainless steel?**
A: Aluminum alloy is lighter, lower-cost, and sufficient for indoor facilities. Stainless steel is the choice if you're installing outdoors or in coastal environments where salt spray and humidity demand higher corrosion resistance.

**Q: Can face recognition be built into an aluminum alloy turnstile?**
A: Yes — several models in the aluminum range run a full Android operating system onboard. Face, card, and QR code readers mount directly into the gate without a separate controller. The ESYBZ LCD+Face model includes a built-in 1920×1080 HD display.

**Q: How many people can an aluminum alloy turnstile handle?**
A: Each lane processes up to 45 people per minute. Actual throughput depends on the credential method — card or QR scanning adds a fraction of a second per entry.

**Q: What are the doors made from?**
A: 10mm acrylic or toughened glass, depending on the series. Both mount on the same aircraft-grade aluminum frame.

**Q: What's the lead time for aluminum alloy turnstiles?**

**Q: Will an aluminum alloy turnstile work with my existing access control system?**
