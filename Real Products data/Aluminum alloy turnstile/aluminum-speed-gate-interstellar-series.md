### SECTION 5 — Feature Deep-Dive Blocks

**H2: A Servo Motor Fast Enough to Disappear Into the Background**

Nobody notices a gate that opens before they've finished their stride. The direct-drive servo motor responds in as little as 0.1 seconds and drives the arm open in under 0.3 seconds, rated for >50 persons per minute per lane. Because the motor drives the arm directly — no gearbox stage in between — operating noise stays under 40 decibels, quiet enough for a museum foyer or a concert hall entrance rather than just a factory gate line.

**H2: A Gate That Fails Safe, Not Just Safe on Paper**

Nobody gets hurt when the arm meets something it shouldn't. If the swing arm's reset is obstructed, the gate triggers reverse braking with three-level torque compensation instead of forcing through — anti-crush protection that's mechanical, not software-only. A sudden collision triggers a buffered rebound plus a reverse stopping force rather than a hard stop. Seven pairs of infrared sensors (ten optional) watch the passage for tailgating, and a direct link to the building's fire alarm system drops the arm to free passage the instant a fire signal cuts power — a code-relevant detail for any procurement officer signing off on emergency egress.

**H2: Manage the Whole Gate Line From a Phone**

An integrator doesn't want to drive to site for every firmware update or fault check. The Interstellar series runs on an ARM industrial control core that supports encrypted parameters, remote firmware upgrades and remote resets, and reports fault codes back to a cloud platform and companion mini-program in real time. Open protocol support — HTTP/HTTPS, WebSocket and MQTT, plus a hardware SDK, device API and UniApp interface — lets a systems house fold the gate into its own building management software instead of running it as a standalone island.

### SECTION 12 — FAQ

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How many people per minute can the Interstellar aluminum speed gate handle?**
A: The Interstellar aluminum speed gate (ES-SIN series) is rated for more than 50 people per minute per lane. That throughput comes from a direct-drive servo motor that opens the arm in under 0.3 seconds.

**Q: What happens to the gate if there's a fire alarm?**
A: The Interstellar series links directly to the building's fire alarm system and drops the arm to free passage the instant power is cut by a fire signal. Release is instantaneous — the manual specifies a zero-second response for emergency egress.

**Q: Is the ES-SIN aluminum speed gate rated for outdoor installation?**
A: The manufacturer's manual lists the ES-SIN series use environment as indoor only, with no IP rating stated. Buyers needing an outdoor or covered-lane aluminum speed gate should ask EASCO about the Xingyao Waterproof series instead.

**Q: What's the difference between the J and JF model codes?**
A: The J1 and J2 model codes are bare gate bodies that connect to an external card reader or remote over RS485, RS232 or IO. The JF1, JF2-S and JF2-D codes add an onboard Android face recognition panel with a 100,000-face capacity and 200–300ms recognition speed.

**Q: Can someone get hurt if they walk into the arm while it's closing?**
A: The gate's reverse-defense mechanism detects an obstruction during the swing-arm reset and applies reverse braking with three-level torque compensation instead of forcing through. A sudden collision triggers a buffered rebound and a reverse stopping force rather than a hard stop.

**Q: What is the warranty on the Interstellar aluminum speed gate?**
A: EASCO's product manual specifies a 12-month warranty from the factory ship date, covering non-human-caused performance faults with free repair. After the warranty period, EASCO charges only labor and transportation, with parts billed at cost.
